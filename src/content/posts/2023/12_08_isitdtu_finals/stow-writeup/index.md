From 8/12/23 to 13/12/23, I had the honour to travel to Danang, Vietnam with NUS Greyhats. We were invited to participate in the ISITDTU CTF Finals.

My Rough Travel Plan was as such

1. Bus from Singapore to Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA)
2. Flight from KLIA to Danang
3. Danang Transit Flight to Ho Chi Minh
4. Flight from Ho Chi Minh back to Singapore

We set off on 8th December from Singapore, cleared Malaysia Immigration, and dropped off at KLIA. We managed to try the McDonalds there. It was interesting to know that they had Nasi Lemak, which is like normal Nasi Lemak but with Fried Chicken! We also passed through the garden at the airport, which was quite nice.

When we reached Da Nang,  we went to eat a good lunch. Afterwards, we dropped off our stuff at the hotel, visited the Guan Yin statue,  and ate frog noodles for dinner.

On our 2nd day, we played the ISITDTU CTF. And surprisingly, we did quite well. We managed to find exploits, write exploits, get flags for exploits and suggest patches to our services. Overall, we managed to get 5th place. For my 1st Attack Defence Capture The Flag Competition, I think that was a decent performance.

We also visited the Dragon Bridge. It was quite fun to see the dragon spit out fire, and you can really feel the heat, even at a distance. 

On our 3rd day, we went to the beach , Marble Mountains and Hoi An. All are great spots. The beach was very clean and calming, a great place to walk around and chill. Marble Mountain was great for walking and hiking. The View when reaching the top is amazing too.

Hoi An was special because of the melting pot of cultures. The boat ride was nice too. The place is a melting pot of Chinese, Vietnamese and Japanese inspiration. The Chinese own 1 side and the bridge, the Vietnamese own another side, and there’s also a Japanese-inspired corner. Quite interesting to see all of this in person.

We had chicken rice for dinner. It was very good, it’s like the chicken rice in Singapore but with more vegetables and lighter of sorts.

On our 4th day, we went to the museum which had a variety of artifacts.

Some other things

1. We ate a fair bit of Bánh Mì, which truly deserves to be called a speciality of Vietnam.
2. The food was amazing all around. Unfortunately, I couldn’t get good photos of all my meals, but I feel Vietnam cuisine is really unique and worth a shot.
3. It was a bit stressful dealing with CourseReg Round 0 during the trip, along with receiving my CS1010 results 😅

Unfortunately, we didn’t really have time to explore more but it was still a fun trip, a good retreat after the stressful finals. Hopefully, we will be able to revisit Danang next year!
