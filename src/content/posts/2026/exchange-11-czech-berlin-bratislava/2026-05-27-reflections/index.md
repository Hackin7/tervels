# Open Hardware Summit -  trip reflections


## Day 1

Open Source Hardware Summit Day 1

Near the Open Source Hardware Summit there's a big pasar malam market, got a lot of things, good vibes

TU Berlin (Math) Campus is quite cool, the colours and hacky vibe is awesome

I think Open Source Hardware Summit itself started out in a very interesting manner

It's a small scale event like Hackaday, but unlike Hackaday 
1. The talks are more geared towards Open Source - some of them have political agendas
    - it's not super bad, I think the way most talks go about it is fine, showcase the tech, and what policywork needs to be done to enable this tech, or what tech enables this policy, kinda interesting
    - but ye there's a political/ leftist? Agenda throughout the conference, when your pin has an option of he/she/they and there's a non negligible amount of they I think you know the demographic it is targeting at. Nothing wrong just different, unlike Hackaday where I feel it's all about the tech, very minor in social stuff

2. there's more art booths and experiences there. 
    - The art showcase tbh isn't much but it is interesting that there even is a section. Some things look cool like VR art, phone storytelling, Chinese pen plotter etc. 
   - unlike hackaday where is mainly sponsor booths, here the booths are about anything - a lot of them involve some artistic creation in some way
Eg. Music, different materials

3. Bio stuff, always fun to see bio engineering at work

4. Not everyone gets a goodie bag, have to pay extra, but it looks good
   - there's no proper food included, only really refreshments, unlike Hackaday with Lunch and Dinner
    - value proposition of attending dips ngl

5. I kinda didn't engage as much this time, partly due to personal circumstances 
   - unlike Hackaday where I could work on the badge hacking contest alongside watching talks which are gonna be recorded, here there's not that much to keep me in
  - there is a space where people can showcase their projects so maybe I'll end up doing that instead on day 2

It feels less techy, more crowded, so it's not as easy for me to get engaged and talk to people
Not that it's bad it's just different

I then went to Burger King to get a proper lunch (then I saved part of my burger and that rolled over into dinner lol)

I can watch the talks on my phone livestreamed too so this is what I meant by nothing keeping much at the event

## Berlin
A lot of people said that the best thing to do in Berlin is to leave😂 but having seen it for myself, I think it's a decently modern place, with a lot of Berlin walls. As someone who wanted to see the other side of the Tembu wall (and understand more about Germany + WW2) it's a decent place

The near deportation experience makes the museum stuff hit harder, no real way to describe it

## Overall
OSHW was an interesting experience, it's kinda like Maker Faire + Hackaday Europe, but with a different crowd. 
1. There weren't any intentional badge hacking showcases and lightning talks, instead you can grab a booth and showcase whatever you want. 
    - I personally prefer lightning talks + badge hacking because it's a condensed way of sharing technical stuff
    - the booths are still quite fun and unique, get to interact more 1-1 but also, and can leave your stuff to showcase also
2. There's a lot more focus on mixed media/ art/ biology stuff, so that's a different scope than Hackaday 
   - more techy performances, techy as in, robocup soccer, custom midi instruments, candles
   - the moth rave (lmao) 
3. It's more bout policy, Open Source Hardware 
    - how to make the thing open source while not going broke
    - you can tell it's a very left wing conference, liberal undertones everywhere
4. Unconference is an interesting idea that I think is not feasible
    - 3d scan a building lmao

As usual, the best part is always showcasing your work, talking to people, doing trades and sales etc.
