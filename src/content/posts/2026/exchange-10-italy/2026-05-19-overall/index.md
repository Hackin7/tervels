Rough thoughts on Hackaday Europe

When I saw that there were no booths webpage or that there was only 1 talk track or that not much was really properly announced I had a suspicion that it's going to be a small conference, and that's basically what I got

I had went to KiCon Asia, in that conference it was not bad but it wasn't really amazing, the talks were ok. The price made up for the food though in a good way. 

In my 1st Hackaday Europe alone I have
1. Taped Out my AOC2025 Day 1 design
2. Ported SSH to the badge (and won a badge hacking contest LOL) 
3. Finally tested Linux on ESP32 and added screen driver support
4. Sold a hfsdr badge + traded various hackbadges, took some selfies
5. Gave my 1st lightning talk which I think went quite well

Here everyone is super technical, everyone is making something, it's frankly one of the most ideal places to just start sharing about your projects. What I didn't expect is that the talks are quite varied, from IC design to retro FPGA computing to music, robotics, cave exploration & computer Vision, SDRs etc. 

It's quite worth the money for the ticket alone, 1 very good badge (+ 1 TinyTapeout ASIC WTF that shits ex) + free crossaint/rice/sandwich/pizza/tiramisu food along the way, and then the community makes it nicer

My expectations were exceeded (slightly), not groundbreaking, but still a very good time. Met new people, met people I seen before, share my stuff, learn new stuff, made stuff

Prob won't go again tho because transport is ex, but if I have sponsorship/ good reason I'll be more than happy to

# Overall

Overall Italy was a really interesting experience and one of the highlight trips I had. Went back in time, and then went forward

1. Bologna - Started out with a modernish Italian City
2. Venice - went back to rivers streams, boats, palace of governance (Doge Palace)
3. Florence - went back to (Roman) Statues and Renaissance Artwork (Uffizi Gallery)
4. Rome - went so far back I saw rubble and rocks😂
5. Milan - started returning to civilisation, city, more proper statues (terracota), and infrastructure (Doumo Cathedral Museum)
6. Lecco - Mountains and Hackaday Europe with all the techy stuff

Cost - 1.5ksgd🤡
- Hackaday Europe - 160 sgd for 2 badges + custom ASIC + Food for the weekend - Super worth
- TinyTapeout - 300sgd🤡
So it's more like 1.1ksgd

It was an experience seeing the ancient stuff at Rome. I have a certain perception of the ancient stuff still standing and that got broken down for me when I saw ROMAN statues in Florence and RUBBLE in Rome. Straight up carved rocks from the ancient past. A lot of amazing stuff was done and worked (colosseum) but a lot of it kinda went to shit over time. And not to mention things like ROMAN statues aren't that far off in history too, being a part of the Renaissance in the 14th century stuffs

The food is really good (pasta+tiramisu is worth trying), the amount of flavour in the pasta is different

My experience at Hackaday Europe was also quite great too, not like DEFCON where I was more of a spectator, but getting the chance to hack and share my stuff (and even lightning talk) on a international stage (no matter how small, might be on Hackaday YouTube channel LOL) 

One thing I didn't expect is to be doing a lot of ASIC stuff, and TinyTapeout, I got it on a whim, but as a former IC Design Intern, and finally having proper information/guidance on how to actually get a chip made for <500sgd

The ancient stuff is indeed fascinating and you can't find it elsewhere
But I do wonder if more things like Hackaday are in SG, I know there's a HackerspaceSG (kinda regret not finding it earlier)

Not really much moralising here, had a fun time, learnt a lot (saw history, saw technical), did a lot, contributed surprisingly a lot too, is basically an ideal trip for me
