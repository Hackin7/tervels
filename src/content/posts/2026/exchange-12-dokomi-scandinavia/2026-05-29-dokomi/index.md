OK time for flashback
The TLDR of my Dokomi experience is that it's just like most anime cons, but a LOT bigger. 

Imagine AFA, that has about 1.5 halls (0.5 from day stage). Now Imagine that on a scale of Embedded World (10 halls). 

It's mainly more of the same kind of stuff in anime cons, exhibits/booths/creators, stages/music, competitions. Its bigger so there's a LOT more, multiple tracks, halls full of booths. Some cases the scale means that more unique things can be done, like hiring more professional musicians, or an AMV room which was a cool idea, or more stages for idol and dances. Some cases it's just expanding on what already works, nothing too unique. 

I felt I didn't get to see much more different than polymanga, but seeing a lot more of it is itself very fun. But I was also quite sad unlike polymanga, because it starts at 1pm (and I arrived at 2pm because of that start timing), I didn't feel I could maximise my value of the event, I wanted to attend more unique local performances of which there were a few
Nevertheless I did what I could, given that I could only get 1 Friday ticket (next time just book tickets then think later ig), and I wanted to balance with jrave

## JRave

I think seeing aiobahn+81 and kors k (IIDX Guy) in person DJing more than made up for the cost

I've went to Golden Mix before, so I liked the music part of the Anime Rave, but didn't really like standing in a packed crowd and being tired. 

Here the hall is big and there are Chairs!!!! And empty space around, I ended up sitting down chilling a lot and vibing to the music. Might have chilled too much and dozed off a bit tho

As and when you want you can stand up and get closer which is definitely the more vibey experience (I did at the last 45min). 

Awesome
1. Anime songs (railgun) , denpa anime songs, IIDX anime songs, old songs, songs I thought most people won't put (True My Heart), Eurobeat, Umamusume
2. Other rave music ig, darude sandstorm, etc.
3. I think aiobahn is a kind of awkward music genius, can tell from the way he speaks, but also when he freestyles. His DJing I remember is denpa vibes which makes sense looking at his output. I find the way he mixes stuff fun, like bad apple and darude sandstorm didn't occur to me, or yunomi hard drop 
4. Kors k DJ is just IIDX vibes, good shit
5. The other DJs were ok, but I think one uses a lot of hololive, umamusume etc. So was nice listening to those songs in a while (yakkaiser was the DJ maybe?) 

Eh
1. I think not everything vibed with me, which is fine, but I guess also that they like hardstyle(?) a lot. They had the hard beat more times than I would have liked, I prefer more trance ig
