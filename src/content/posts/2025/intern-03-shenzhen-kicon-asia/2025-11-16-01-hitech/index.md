# The China Hi-Tech Fair

A lot of things but I feel also might be too industrial or out of my scope to fully appreciate it. So usually I'll speedrun it and see the blink blink fancy stuff. 

The robots were cool to see tho, component suppliers were always quite fun, tech is always cool

I think because its far removed from the rest of the world, really focusing on China stuff, a bit hard to relate for me. It's good tho. I'll say it's like Music China, it has 16 halls or so.


# Huaqiangbei

It was 3.30pm. My train was at 8.21pm.

It's not my 1st time in huaqiangbei and the electronics I wanted to get are closed so I went around to just look look see see.

I saw the top floor of seg electronics mall, saw some other tech stuff and the old shops I went to last time etc. 

I'll say to me it's like Sim Lim but cheaper. Taobao is definitely cheaper but here you can test the stuff

I didn't know huaqiangbei had a food street so I went there for a while to eat a nice dinner (and not like fast food)

Then I went to the train station, I mistook another train for mine lol (thankfully got stopped early)

# Overall

Overall my Shenzhen trip was not exactly too amazing.
It was nice, and as an engineer who does hobby projects I'll say all tinkers have to come here once in their lives, and this is my 2nd time. 

Idk imo it's so hard to find people able to geek out about maker tech stuff together. I'm in a tech company and even so I don't find the passion in a lot of people around me. Or maybe they're jaded idk. 

I'll say maybe I had too high expectations for KiCon and CHTF, but I wouldn't say I regret it, I say they were good events till. I learnt something from KiCon and got good food, Hitech was interesting like Music China also. Maker Faire Shenzhen was quite fun, I'll say it finally felt more like a proper maker faire, compared to Shanghai. 

What's heartening about all these is that Singapore does have/had all of this, we have conferences, we had ROSCON, SWITCH, we had a Sim Lim, we have a Maker Faire before. And imo I'll say apart from the scale we aren't all too far off. The events in China haven't exactly blew my mind off, in fact I don't think any event blew my mind off that much, (except now looking back on it, BlackHat and DEFCON, we actually can't compete)

I really want to see if we can slowly bring it to Singapore, Div0 has given me huge hope that we can slowly head in that direction, not just for Cyber but as a whole
