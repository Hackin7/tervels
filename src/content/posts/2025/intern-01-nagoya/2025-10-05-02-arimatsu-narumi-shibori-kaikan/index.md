# Shibori Tie-Dyeing Museum in Arimatsu

I went here for a more down to earth experience than the Tokugawa Art museum etc and that's definitely what I got

The 1st floor is selling the crafts made with this Shibori technique, some of it is quite expensive (100 SGD+ for a shirt), but there are some 770yen handkerchiefs etc. I bought a handkerchief and some 400yen cloth thing for my mint tin can (After the 2nd floor exhibition)

I think the Shibori museum itself doesn't have that much but if you delve deep it is quite interesting.

What it has is a
1. Movie explaining what Shibori is (no filming) 
2. 2 people demonstrating Shibori and explaining
3. Some things about the different Shibori techniques

The people here are very friendly, when I first entered the top floor, one of them was speaking very warmly in English, saying that she went to Chile to show off this, and apparently they learnt it themselves there and did not know Shibori, that's quite insane. She said something that it's all about the people and I really agree with this. There's an upcoming thing in Swiss in sep? Idk

## Arimatsu Shibori Movie

1. Design
   - on pattern
   - poke holes
   - then put some dye through the holes
2. Kukuri (tying)
    - prevent dye from penetration
    - over 100 various techniques, 1 technique by 1 person,
    - takes a great deal of time
    - "you are not taught. it was like a game in our yohth"
3. Senshoku (textile dying) 
    - soaking fabric into dye liquid. Becomes very heacy
    - small mistakes ruin the process
    - special ways to dye specific fabric
    - can be 100 ways to dye 100 fabrics or Shibori
 4. Taking our the stitches
    - tight stitches to prevent oenetration, inspect for leftover stitches to sustain quality
5. Finishing
    - unoshi - a type of steam iron
    - steam applied, wrinkles taken out, length is fixed all my band

Younger artists bring new generation stuff and trends, while basing skills on tradition

Here are the different kinds

They are very friendly and nice, even though they don't understand or speak English they guide me through how it works, really interesting

I think on other days they have workshops where people can make their own stuff

After this I bought the stuff and I think it's worth to support
